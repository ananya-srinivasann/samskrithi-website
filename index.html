Samskrithi School of Dance
Kalakshetra-style Bharatanatyam School in the Greater Boston Area

Bharatanatyam is a classical dance from Southern India, where it was performed as an offering to the Hindu Gods as part of the daily temple rituals. Initially called 'Sadir', the dance took the name 'Bharatanatyam' when it moved from the temples to the proscenium stage in the mid-20th century. The dance also has references in the ancient Tamil epic Silappadikaram from the 2nd century CE as a highly refined performance art and is considered the oldest classical tradition of India. The dance employs compelling stories from a vast resource of Hindu mythology, and also displays variations of geometric movements and patterns that can range from slow and subtle to crisp and bold.

We aim to promote the rich heritage of Bharatanatyam by offering classical training that allows students to develop their skill, passion, and respect for the arts.
